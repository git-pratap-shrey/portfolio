About Me

I’m an AI-focused backend developer who builds systems that actually work in real-world scenarios, not just demos. I’m especially interested in how large language models behave when you put real constraints around them—where retrieval, structure, and reliability matter more than just generation.

I built uniAI, a study assistant designed around how students actually prepare for exams. It answers strictly from syllabus and notes instead of guessing, which pushed me to think deeply about retrieval, routing, and evaluation. I also developed a LeetCode Notes Automation system that turns problem-solving workflows into structured revision notes, exploring data scraping and how LLMs can produce consistent, repeatable outputs in a multi-user, scalable setup. Alongside this, I worked on Aurora Sentinels, a smart surveillance system where I gained hands-on experience with vision-enabled systems and built semantic search powered by vision-language models to help operators find relevant moments from hours of footage.

What I enjoy most is turning real-world ideas into structured systems—designing pipelines that are predictable, debuggable, and genuinely useful. I’m still exploring this space, but I care a lot about building things that are practical, not just impressive on paper.
